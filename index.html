About Me

The story behind the data

My career in analytics began at Bharat Electronics Limited, a public-sector defence electronics organisation, where I was responsible for making sense of large operational datasets and translating them into management dashboards. It was an environment that valued precision and structure and it shaped a core belief I have carried since: data is only valuable when it drives a decision. Reporting for its own sake is just noise. I was fortunate enough that I had supported analytics and technical(testing) operations for EVM & VVPAT systems and on-field technical assistance during the 2019 General Election.

At Uniworth Enterprises, I moved into a data and reporting function where the challenge was different but the principle remained the same. Business stakeholders had pressing questions, the data held the answers, and closing that gap was my responsibility. I redesigned reporting pipelines, automated time-intensive manual processes, and conducted a customer churn analysis that influenced a fundamental change in the organisation's retention strategy. That last piece of work mattered most not because of the technical execution, but because the analysis led somewhere.

At Teleperformance in the United Kingdom, I work in a faster, more complex environment customer operations at scale, where data quality is inconsistent, business needs shift quickly, and the margin for imprecision is low. I have reduced operational response times by 18%, rebuilt the reporting infrastructure to provide real-time visibility to leadership, and consistently served as the link between what customer behaviour data reveals and what product and operations teams need to act on it. The environments have changed across each role. The approach has not.

01 — FEATURED · PRODUCT ANALYTICS
User Engagement Analysis
Using Yelp Public Data
Understanding what drives platform engagement and where businesses lose user attention.
PythonPandasSQLTableau
Business Problem
Platform businesses rely on engagement loops to retain users but most don't know which specific behaviours predict long-term retention versus one-time visits. This project set out to identify the engagement signals that actually matter, using Yelp's public dataset as a proxy for any platform with user-generated content.
Analytical Approach
Segmented users by activity levels and recency. Built engagement cohorts to track drop-off patterns. Applied correlation analysis between review frequency, rating behaviour, and return visit likelihood. Mapped the customer journey from first interaction to high-value user status.
Key Insights & Business Recommendations
Users who left 3+ reviews in the first 30 days showed 2× retention rate onboarding incentives should target this behaviour.
Businesses with response rates above 70% had significantly higher repeat engagement product teams should prioritise owner reply features.
Photo uploads correlated more strongly with review engagement than star ratings content type matters for feature prioritisation.
Weekend activity spikes were 3× higher but conversion to return visits was lower suggesting opportunity for post-weekend re-engagement nudges.
↑ Informs product engagement roadmap · Retention feature prioritisation

02 — FEATURED · REVENUE & HYPOTHESIS TESTING
Maximising Revenue Analysis
with Hypothesis Testing
Using statistical rigour to challenge assumptions and identify real revenue levers.
PythonSciPyNumPyPower BI
Business Problem
Sales and revenue decisions are often based on gut feel or surface-level trends. This project asked: which pricing, segment, and timing factors genuinely drive revenue? And which assumptions don't hold up under statistical scrutiny?
Analytical Approach
Ran A/B style hypothesis tests on pricing strategies, customer segments, and promotional timing. Applied t-tests and chi-square tests to validate which differences were statistically significant versus noise. Built a decision framework for future revenue experiments.
Key Insights & Business Recommendations
Mid-tier pricing segment showed statistically significantly higher LTV than premium counter to the assumed revenue hierarchy.
Promotional timing in the first 48 hours post-sign-up had 2.3× the conversion rate of campaigns sent at day 7.
Regional performance gaps that appeared large were shown to be statistically insignificant budget reallocation was not warranted.
Recommended structured experimentation framework to replace ad hoc revenue decisions going forward.
↑ Prevents misallocation of marketing budget · Grounds revenue decisions in evidence

03 — FEATURED · MARKETING & A/B TESTING
Marketing Campaign Analysis
& A/B Testing
Determining which campaign strategies convert and why the others don't.
PythonPandasStatisticsTableau
Business Problem
Marketing teams regularly run multiple campaigns simultaneously without a rigorous way to attribute performance. This leads to budget waste and missed optimisation opportunities. The objective was to isolate campaign effectiveness, identify audience segments that respond, and recommend where to invest.
Analytical Approach
Designed and analysed A/B test results across multiple campaign variants. Controlled for confounding variables (seasonality, audience overlap). Applied conversion funnel analysis to identify where users dropped off. Segmented results by demographic and behavioural attributes.
Key Insights & Business Recommendations
Email campaigns outperformed paid social by 34% in conversion rate budget reallocation recommended with projected savings.
25–34 age segment had 2× click-through but half the conversion funnel optimisation needed post-click, not pre-click.
Subject line variant B increased open rates by 18% small copy changes had outsized impact at scale.
Identified optimal send window (Tuesday 9–11am) that increased engagement by 22% vs overall average.
↑ Direct campaign ROI improvement · Channel budget reallocation
